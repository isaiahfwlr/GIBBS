Our Mission

The Gibbs Lab is dedicated to understanding the fundamental mechanisms of microbial life. Our research focuses on how environmental microbes adapt to changes in metal content and nutrient availability.

Specifically, we are interested in exploring how changes in metal availability impact the ribosome's ability to sense and respond to these changes, providing insights into survival strategies and ecosystem processes.

  • Molecular Microbiology
  • Bacterial Adaptation
  • Education & Mentorship

Research Topics

Exploring adaptation and regulation through three core pillars.

Molecular Biology

Understanding how environmental microbes adapt to changes in metal content by studying the molecular mechanisms involved, specifically the ribosome's sensing ability.

Microbial Studies

Exploring the process of adaptation to nutrient availability in individual isolates as well as entire microbiomes to understand survival strategies.

Genetic Insights

Investigating mechanisms of genetic regulation in bacteria, both at the transcriptional and translational levels, to understand survival in diverse surroundings.

Meet the Team

Dr. Michelle R. Gibbs

Principal Investigator

My career in microbiology began at Miami University in Ohio, where I studied pathogenesis in Mycoplasma. I earned my PhD from Ohio State University, focusing on the role of translational GTPases in ribosome assembly. I completed two postdoctoral fellowships at NIH and UCLA as an IRACDA fellow. Throughout my career, I have taught courses in microbiology, biochemistry, and biology at Wittenberg University and California State University, Los Angeles. I am broadly interested in microbial adaptation to nutrient stress, genetic regulation, and ribosome assembly and function. I started my position as Assistant Research Professor in January 2024.
